A Calming Anxiety

May 2, 2026


I've recently found myself oscillating between two poles: anxious and calm, with not much in between.


Only the paranoid survive

- Andy Grove


Perform your duty equipoised, abandoning all attachment to success or failure. Such equanimity is called yoga.

- Krishna, The Bhagavad Gita


How do I reconcile both of these philosophies?


I need Andy Grove's paranoia to remind myself that the path to greatness is not guaranteed. If I want to do something difficult, to solve the unsolved, I need to consistently remind myself of the competitive dynamics of the world. If I don't build it, someone else will. I need to remember this at all times in order to win.


Constant anxiety is burdensome, however. If I let it, it will crush my ambitions and consume my soul. This is especially detrimental when trying to get specific things done. When confronted with a well-specified task or problem, it's important for me to be able to approach it with the latter philosophy. In my experience, the best work I've done has come from what feels like a meditative state.


When all surrounding anxiety is dissolved, and the only object in the world is my duty. Not when I'm fussed about making it work by a made-up deadline or scared about it never working, but when the pursuit of the task itself is all I'm paying attention to.


I'll probably continue to move between these two poles. What matters is figuring out how, and when, to switch between them.
