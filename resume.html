Professional Experience

Motional - Software Engineer

May 2022 - July 2024 | Boston, MA

As a Software Engineer at Motional, I led the development and maintenance of a data pipeline that changed our approach to incident management and analysis for our Autonomous Vehicle fleet. My primary focus was developing machine learning models and heuristic-based methods to identify and classify failures in autonomous vehicles. These models operated on vast amounts of data from multiple autonomy subsystems, including but not limited to: planned trajectories, vehicle kinematics, object detections, and predicted trajectories for detected objects.

I architected a scalable AWS cloud services infrastructure (databases, message queues, automated ingestion services) enabling triage of approximately 10,000 autonomous vehicle incidents daily. To ensure long-term reliability, I automated model improvement through periodic polling, re-training, and monitoring. Throughout my time on this team, I contributed to the development of software standards and CI/CD pipelines, further enhancing team efficiency and product quality.

Skills: Machine Learning, AWS, SQL, REST APIs, GraphQL, CI/CD, Data Engineering, Python, Cloud Infrastructure

Motional - Robotics Research Intern, Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)

August 2021 - May 2022 | Boston, MA

As a Robotics Research Intern on the Human-Robot Interaction team at Motional, I studied methods to socially optimize the behavior of Autonomous Vehicles. In general, the formulation of the objective function of AV research is to find optimal methods to safely and efficiently traverse through roads designed for humans to drive on. A lot of this conventional research misses out on some of the implicit behaviors humans exhibit while interacting with other human road users. My research objective was to decode some of these implicit behaviors, and to find ways to integrate them within Motional's existing autonomy stack.

For example, we studied the interactions of AVs and pedestrians as they cross the street, behavior when interacting with first responders, and the intuitiveness of lane change intent signaling. As part of this internship, I co-authored numerous papers. These are listed in the publications section below.

Skills: Human-Robot Interaction, Autonomous Vehicles, Research, Machine Learning, Python, ROS, Academic Writing

Human-Robot Interaction Lab, Tufts University - Robotics Research Assistant

May 2021 - August 2021 | Medford, MA

As a Robotics Research Assistant at the Human-Robot Interaction Lab at Tufts University, I focused on enhancing the social intelligence of mobile robots in human environments. Particularly, I worked to build a Socially Aware Path Navigation system that explicitly considered the violation of personal space boundaries within its cost function.

This involved developing C++ code to modify the representation of human agents in ROS costmaps, and experimenting with various cost function formulations, tailoring them to different planning algorithms (A*, RRT* and more) to find the most effective approach.

Additionally, I contributed to the lab's efforts in context-aware robot behavior by creating a ROS module that could influence robot navigation through predefined zones. This system, built on the ROS server-client model, allowed for dynamic adjustment of robot behavior in marked polygon areas, enabling more nuanced responses to different environmental contexts.

Skills: Robotics, C++, ROS, Path Planning, Human-Robot Interaction, Research, Python
