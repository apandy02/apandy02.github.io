Hi there, my name is Aryaman Pandya. I'm a Robotics Software Engineer with a passion for Human-Centered Design. We're currently entering an era where intelligent machines increasingly inhabit our digital and physical worlds, and my goal is to help these systems adopt intuitive, human-centered behaviors. My primary interests lie at the intersection of learning-based systems and human-computer/robot interaction.

Most recently, I joined Wayve to build observability systems for our autonomous fleet. Previously I worked on large-scale machine learning systems at Motional, focusing on the detection and classification of complex failures in autonomous vehicles. Additionally, I've contributed to several research projects within Human-Robot Interaction (HRI), including work at the Human-Robot Interaction Lab at Tufts University and collaborative research with industry leaders in HRI for autonomous vehicles.

I grew up in Mumbai, India, where I obtained my IB Diploma from Oberoi International School. I then moved to Medford, Massachusetts, earning my Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering from Tufts University.

While robotics and machine learning define my professional pursuits, my approach to problem-solving was fundamentally shaped by physics, my first academic passion.

Outside of academics, I competed nationally in squash in India, continuing the sport at the varsity level during college. Additionally, I'm a lifelong martial artist, actively practicing karate, Muay Thai, and Jiu-Jitsu.
