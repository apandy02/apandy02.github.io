About Me

I'm Aryaman Pandya, a Software Engineer with a passion for Human-Centered Design and experience in building robust machine learning applications. I previously worked on large scale machine learning systems at Motional. My models were responsible for the detection and classification of complex Autonomous Vehicle failures.

I grew up in Mumbai, India where I obtained my IB Diploma at the Oberoi International School. I then moved to Medford, Massachusetts where I earned my Bachelor's of Science in Computer Engineering at Tufts University.

As an engineer, I'm excited by the prospect of taking cutting edge intelligent systems, and crafting them into technically sound products that enhance the human experience. Over the past few years, I've had the opportunity to contribute to a number of research projects in the field of Human-Robot Interaction. This includes work with the Human-Robot Interaction Lab at Tufts University, and cross-collaborative research with leading researchers in the field of HRI for Autonomous Vehicles.

As a teenager (and younger) I competed on the national squash circuit in India, a sport I pursued at the varsity level in college as well. I also am a lifelong martial artist, practicing karate, muay thai, and jiu jitsu.
