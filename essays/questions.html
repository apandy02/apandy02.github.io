Questions

March 9, 2026


What does a country of geniuses in a data center unlock? Do the geniuses solve problems that seem intractable to the rest of us? Or are they upper bound by some fundamental limitation of human-like intelligence?


Is the marginal product of developing such a country of geniuses in a data center greater than anything else we could be working on? Or should we be starting new scientific projects that some of these new geniuses can contribute to?


There are, of course, physical constraints. There are also regulatory and diffusion-based ones. Which friction requires the most lubrication? Which is most worth trying to ease? Which ones will resolve themselves as we ascend the ladder of intelligence?


How should we think about the free market when corporations own labor that is limited only by access to energy and compute? Can we avoid monopolistic scenarios? Or are we doomed to continue our descent down the slope of consolidation?


Will synthetic agents need their own tokens of exchange? Perhaps their own economy, separate from ours?


These are some of the many big questions we are confronted with at this moment. These are also some of the ones I do not feel confident in my answers to. I expect it will become progressively more imperative to answer these and more in the coming years.
